*Divination cantrip*
**Casting Time:** :: 1 action
**Range:** :: 30.0 feet
**Components:** :: V, S
**Duration:** :: 1 minutes

You subtly displace an object’s presence in the perceptions of others, as though its existence had been poorly recorded.

Choose one unattended, nonmagical object within range no larger than a book. For the duration, the object is visually and cognitively deemphasized.

Creatures other than you who perceive the object must succeed on an Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC to consciously notice it. On a failure, the creature treats the object as overlooked, misplaced, or irrelevant to its attention, though the object remains physically present.

The effect ends immediately if the object is handled, moved, or directly interacted with, or if a creature is explicitly directed to examine the object.

This spell does not render the object invisible, suppress sound, or prevent accidental contact. It merely encourages the mind to disregard what is plainly there.

You impose a highly specific transformation upon the boundary between humanoid and fowl.

Choose one humanoid creature or one Tiny beast of the bird family that you can see within range.

Unwilling Target

An unwilling humanoid must make a Constitution saving throw.

- Failure: The creature is transformed for the duration.
- Success: The spell has no effect.

A transformed creature may repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.

Willing Target

A willing creature is transformed without a saving throw.

A bird targeted by this spell is always considered willing.

⸻

Forms

- Humanoid to Rooster
The creature transforms into a Tiny beast (rooster) with:

– 1 hit point
– Speed 10 ft
– Peck attack (1 damage, no proficiency)
– No ability to speak or cast spells

The creature retains its alignment, personality, and awareness, but its mental ability scores are replaced by those of the beast. It behaves as a normal, notably aggressive rooster at the GM’s discretion.

- Rooster to Humanoid
The creature transforms into a Medium humanoid of a general appearance chosen by the caster.

The humanoid:

– Possesses basic clothing
– Cannot recall its time as a bird
– Has no equipment or class features

⸻

Duration & Reversal

The caster may end the spell early as an action.

Casting this spell on a creature already transformed by it immediately reverts the target.

⸻

Blood Pact Amplification

If the spell is cast using a Vitality + Unique Offering blood pact, the transformation becomes permanent.

A permanent transformation may only be reversed by effects capable of undoing major transmutations, such as Greater Restoration, Remove Curse, or Wish, subject to GM adjudication.

- Aggressive: Will charge anything larger than it.
- Peck: Melee Weapon Attack, +3 to hit, 1 slashing damage.

#### Abhaaldrac’s Unmaking Descent
*9th-level Evocation*
**Casting Time:** :: 1 action
**Range:** :: Self (300.0-foot radius)
**Components:** :: V, S
**Duration:** :: 24 hourss

You call upon Abhaaldrac, the World Destroyer who drifts beyond the Far Cosmos, and invite a fragment of its attention to fall upon the world. The moment its presence presses against reality, the Loom begins to tear.

Across the affected region, existence destabilizes violently. Gravity bends toward impossible vectors, structures shear and realign, and the Veil fractures as alien geometries intrude. The land does not merely break, it is rewritten according to principles that do not belong to this world.

Anomalous entities and cosmic hazards manifest spontaneously, acting without pattern, allegiance, or mercy. Spellcraft within the area becomes dangerously unstable as Abhaaldrac’s influence distorts intent and outcome, causing magic to misfire, mutate, or recoil upon its caster.

At its highest invocation, Abhaaldrac does not withdraw. The descent completes, and the region becomes a permanent scar upon reality, a place where the Far Cosmos presses close and the world forever remembers that it was once seen by something vast enough to end it.
Point of origin and size: Choose a point you can see within 300 feet. The effect fills a 1-mile-radius region centered on that point. This is a regional phenomenon, not a combat template.
Duration: The region persists for 24 hours. If cast at 10th level, the region becomes permanent and functions as a quarantined anomaly site (DM discretion on containment and escalation).
Region traits: The region is considered planar-thin and geomagnetically unstable. The following effects apply throughout the region unless a specific hazard result says otherwise.
Spatial shear: Straight-line movement remains possible, but structures, corridors, and sightlines may no longer align. Doors can open into non-adjacent rooms. Climbing routes can lead somewhere else than expected.
Gravity lurches: At intermittent moments, gravity “pulls” toward a temporary vector. Creatures can be knocked prone, lifted, or slammed into nearby surfaces depending on the hazard roll.
Veil fractures: The region intermittently produces anomalous entities and hazards. They are not summoned allies. They act according to their own instincts or the hazard entry.
Hazard cadence: At the start of each hour, and whenever the DM wants to emphasize escalation (entering a landmark, casting a spell, loud battle, opening a sealed door), roll once on the Abhaaldrac Hazard Table. The result affects a sub-area chosen by the DM, typically a 300 to 1,000 foot zone, or follows the party if you want it to feel predatory.
Unstable spellcraft: Whenever a creature casts a spell of 1st level or higher inside the region, they must make a spellcasting ability check against DC 15 + the spell’s level.
- Success: The spell functions normally.
- Failure: Roll once on the Spellcraft Misfire Table. The spell slot is still expended.
- Rituals: A ritual cast within the region automatically triggers a misfire roll at completion unless the caster succeeds on the above check.
- Counterspell and Dispel Magic: These also require the check. On a failure, they misfire instead of resolving normally.
Safe pockets: The DM may place rare “still points” where hazard cadence is reduced (for example, hazard rolls only every 6 hours). These are valuable objectives and should feel earned.
Abhaaldrac Hazard Table (d12)
	1.	Gravity Lurch. Each creature in the affected sub-area makes a Strength saving throw (DC 18) or is knocked prone and slides 30 feet toward the current vector.
	2.	Structural Shear. The ground and walls “realign.” Difficult terrain for 10 minutes. Doors, corridors, and stairways in the sub-area no longer connect as mapped.
	3.	Vector Inversion. Ranged weapon attacks and spell attacks in the sub-area have disadvantage until the next hazard roll as trajectories bend unpredictably.
	4.	Veil Splinters. 1d4 anomalous entities manifest in the sub-area. Use any appropriate stat blocks, but they are hostile or indifferent, never friendly by default.
	5.	Chromatic Pressure. Creatures must make a Constitution save (DC 18) or take 4d8 psychic damage and have disadvantage on Wisdom checks for 1 hour.
	6.	Null Draft. Magical light is suppressed in the sub-area for 1 hour. Nonmagical flames sputter and dim.
	7.	Echo Weather. A localized storm of particulate debris, ash, or metallic dust. Heavily obscured beyond 30 feet for 10 minutes.
	8.	Inertial Snap. Each creature makes a Dexterity save (DC 18) or takes 6d6 bludgeoning damage as the environment “catches up” with their motion.
	9.	Bleached Silence. Sound is muffled. Creatures have disadvantage on Perception checks that rely on hearing for 1 hour.
	10.	Horizon Fold. The sub-area becomes subtly larger inside than outside. Travel time doubles for 1 hour.
	11.	Predatory Geometry. The sub-area produces a moving hazard that pursues the nearest creature for 10 minutes (treat as a hazard that forces Dex saves each round or each minute outside combat).
	12.	The Loom Tears. The next spell cast in the region automatically triggers a misfire roll, even if the check succeeds.

Spellcraft Misfire Table (d12)
	1.	Recoil. The caster takes 3d6 force damage per spell level.
	2.	Inversion. The spell’s damage type becomes psychic, or if it was psychic, it becomes force.
	3.	Offset Targeting. The spell originates from a point 30 feet in a random direction from the intended point.
	4.	Overreach. The spell affects one additional random valid target in range.
	5.	Underreach. The spell’s range is halved. If already touch, it fails harmlessly but still expends the slot.
	6.	Backwash. The caster is affected as if they were also a target, if the spell can legally target them.
	7.	Mutation. The spell gains a cosmetic anomaly, and a minor mechanical twist chosen by the DM (for example, difficult terrain, a loud shriek, or a lingering stench).
	8.	Dead Channel. The spell fizzles. No effect. Slot expended.
	9.	Double Cast. The spell resolves normally, then immediately repeats against the same targets with half effect.
	10.	Wild Exchange. Swap a key parameter, chosen by the DM: line becomes cone, sphere becomes cube, or range becomes self with area centered on caster if applicable.
	11.	Arcane Fracture. The caster cannot cast spells of that level or higher for 1 minute.
	12.	Call Attention. Roll immediately on the Hazard Table, then apply the hazard at the spell’s point of origin.

#### Create Spell
*9th-level Transmutation*
**Casting Time:** :: 1 minutes
**Range:** :: Self
**Components:** :: None
**Duration:** :: Instantaneous

You attempt the most perilous act of arcane mastery: the forging of a previously unknown spellform. Through intense concentration and esoteric procedure, you impose a new magical working upon the fabric of the world.

When you cast this spell, you design a new spell effect. The nature, magnitude, and limits of the created spell are determined by the GM, who is the final arbiter of its viability, scope, and consequences.

As guidance for adjudication:

- Cantrip / 1st–3rd Level Effects: Minor, narrow, or situational workings.
- 4th–6th Level Effects: Significant combat or utility magic, typically with constraints or risks.
- 7th–8th Level Effects: Exceptional workings involving control, transformation, or life-altering influence, always accompanied by serious drawbacks or instability.
- 9th-Level Effects: World-shaping magic comparable only to the most extreme known spells.

The created spell becomes part of your personal magical repertoire if deemed stable. Only one created spell may remain stable in this manner at any time.

Attempts to maintain multiple created spells may result in failure, collapse, or unforeseen magical consequences at the GM’s discretion.

⸻

Risks & Consequences

The act of forging a spell is inherently dangerous. The GM may impose consequences appropriate to the magnitude of the effect, including but not limited to:

- Exhaustion or physical debilitation
- Psychic or force backlash
- Memory erosion or cognitive distortion
- Permanent scars of magical strain
- Unintended side effects or flaws

Failure may produce partial, distorted, or hazardous outcomes.
